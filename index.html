Your Short Guide to Living Healthy

You've been told by your Doctor that you need to live a healthier lifestyle. But what does that really mean? With so many differing opinions, how do you know where to get started? That is why I created this short and easily digestable guide to help those starting out in their health journey.

Find Out More Below

4 Areas to Focus


When starting your health and wellness journey, it is helpful to think about pinpointing into the key areas below.

Intellectual

Continually learning new things to sharpen your mind.

Physical

Get up and get moving each day, keeping your body strong.

Mental

Giving yourself quiet time to refelct, ponder, and plan.

Spiritual

Faith to center your life and keep things in perspective.

Meditation
Meditation is a great way that you can get started on your wellness journey. Starting out with meditation is super easy, requiring no prior knowledge or fancy equipment. Plus you can meditate whenever it is convenient for you! To start, close your eyes. Clear your thoughts by focusing on steady breaths and a singular emotion, such as gratitude. Relax, and find stillness in the moment. Check out youtube for helpful guided meditations!
Drinking Water
Drinking water is a small change you can make that will have a significant impact on your life. Not only will you have more energy, drinking water may help with increased digestion and nutrient absorption. A few simple things you can do is replace sugary beverages with water, drink a big glass after waking up, or drink water with each meal.
Get Outside
One of the most under rated improvements we can make in our life is spending more time outside. With sedentary jobs and working from home, it is important to prioritize being in nature. Even if you aren't a fan of the great outdoors making simple changes such as going for a walk, watching the sunrise, or admiring the trees will have significant impact.
Enough Sleep
There are a small minority of people that get enough sleep in the world. We have a high pressure culture of focusing on productivity and 'grinding.' Unfortunatly this practice comes at the cost of our health. Prioritizing our sleep actually saves time! How? With a sharpened mind we will be able to increase productivity and focus during waking hours rather than dozing off, ultimately getting more done. Try getting just one extra hour of sleep tonight and see how you feel!
Cooking
The saying 'you are what you eat' merits some truth. Cooking is one of the biggest lifestyle changes someone can make to increase their health. By cooking at home, your food is more nutrient dense, satisfying, and all around cleaner. Unfortunatly, it is a big time commitment. Start with replacing just 2 convenience meals with home cooked ones, making enough for leftovers! You are sure to feel the difference.
Exercise
It is time to get moving! Sometimes people hear exercise and internally groan. But exercise doesn't have to be an intense, sweaty workout. Something as simple as stretching or going for a walk will pay huge dividends towards your health goals. It matters less what you do, and more getting your body moving.
